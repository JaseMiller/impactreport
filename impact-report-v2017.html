Refugee mothers are often invisible. Being the primary caregiver of their children leaves them at home without access to a community or opportunities to learn and practice English. At the Homework Club in Rockwood (Portland, Oregon), our tutoring center for refugee and immigrant families, mothers can come with their children to have some adult interactions, practice English and simply been seen and valued.

In Uzhgorod, Ukraine, thousands of Roma young people are trapped within their squatter camps because they see no hope of escaping. All they see ahead of them is a life of sifting through garbage, cleaning streets, begging, or stealing. Only 20% of the Roma children go to school because the parents see no hope of them escaping as well and education is not necessary for those tasks. We recently partnered with Roma Integration to start a pilot Roma youth mentoring course. We spent ten weeks with ten young Roma and introduced them to professional Roma doctors, businessmen, politicians, and pastors who had escaped the poverty of the camps. This instilled in them a hope that they could escape as well as these mentors shared what they had to do, like get a good education, to get out of the camps.

In Carrick (Pittsburgh, PA) our neighbor Meg is 21 and has pretty much been on his own since he was eight. His mom left when he was three. His dad remarried and encouraged his father and her daughter to shun Meg. He slept at different people’s houses in the refugee camp in Nepal and often disappeared for days at a time.

Jesus found Meg when he was 15 and his life was flipped upside down. He still had little relationship with his family but when we moved to Carrick in 2015 Meg had just moved in across the street. He is at our place several days a week and often stays until late in the evenings. Meg is discipling us. We are discipling him. He has become our surrogate son and he is not invisible. We are in the process of trying to see Meg moved into formal leadership with ITeams and join our team as he has a clear call to ministry on his life.
